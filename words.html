What Are Hotspots?

Hotspots are places around the world with a significant amount of biodiversity. There are animals and plants here that cannot be found anywhere else in the world. At least 0.5% of the vascular plants living there must be endemics, which is a species which is unique to a specific zone, habitat type, or area. These species are indigenous to an area and cannot be found anywhere else. In addition to this, to qualify as a hotspot it must have lost at least 70% of its primary vegetarian living in the area due to habitat loss or other causes. There are a total of 36 hotspots in the world. These hotspots are the home of almost 60% of all the plants, birds, mammals, reptiles, and amphibian species in the world. A very high share of these animals are also endemics. Hotspots are irreplaceable places and most be protected in order to ensure the survival of the species.
